## Overview

A VPN kill switch is a security feature that blocks your internet connection if the VPN disconnects unexpectedly.

This helps prevent your real IP address from being exposed.

## Why a Kill Switch Matters

When using a VPN, your traffic goes through an encrypted tunnel.

But if the VPN disconnects, your device may automatically return to your normal internet connection.

This can expose:

- Your real IP address
- Your location
- Your browsing activity
- Your app traffic

A kill switch helps prevent this leak.

## How a Kill Switch Works

A kill switch monitors your VPN connection.

If the VPN drops, it blocks internet access until the VPN reconnects.

Simple flow:

1. VPN connected.
2. Internet traffic goes through VPN.
3. VPN disconnects unexpectedly.
4. Kill switch blocks internet.
5. VPN reconnects.
6. Internet access resumes.

## When You Should Enable It

You should enable kill switch if you care about:

- Public Wi-Fi privacy
- Torrent privacy
- Remote work security
- Avoiding IP leaks
- Always-on VPN protection

## When You May Not Need It

Some users may keep it off if they prefer internet access over privacy.

For example, if your VPN disconnects during a video call, a kill switch may stop the call.
