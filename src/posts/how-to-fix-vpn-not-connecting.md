## Overview

Sometimes a VPN does not connect, disconnects quickly, or connects but the internet stops working.

This guide explains the most common VPN connection problems and how to fix them.

## Step 1: Check Your Internet Connection

Before blaming the VPN, check if your normal internet works.

Disconnect the VPN and open a website.

If the website does not load without VPN, your internet connection is the problem.

Try:

- Restarting Wi-Fi
- Switching to mobile data
- Restarting your router
- Testing another website

## Step 2: Change VPN Server

A VPN server may be offline, overloaded, or blocked.

Open your VPN app and select another server.

Try:

- A nearby server
- A different country
- A low-ping server
- A backup server from your provider

## Step 3: Update Your Subscription

If you use apps like Hiddify, Clash, or Sing-box, update your subscription.

Your server list may be outdated.

Steps:

1. Open the VPN app.
2. Go to your profile.
3. Tap Update or Refresh.
4. Try connecting again.

## Step 4: Restart the VPN App

Close the VPN app completely and open it again.

On mobile, remove it from recent apps.

On desktop, close it from the taskbar or system tray.

Then try connecting again.

## Step 5: Restart Your Device

A simple restart can fix network conflicts.

Restart your phone, tablet, or computer and try again.

## Step 6: Check VPN Permission

On Android and iOS, VPN permission is required.

If permission was denied, the VPN cannot connect.

Open the app again and allow VPN permission when asked.

## Step 7: Try Another Protocol

Some VPN apps allow protocol selection.

Try switching between:

- WireGuard
- OpenVPN TCP
- OpenVPN UDP
- VLESS
- Trojan
- Hysteria
- Reality
- Sing-box profiles

Some networks block certain protocols, so another protocol may work better.

## Step 8: Check Date and Time

Incorrect date or time can break secure connections.

Set your device date and time to automatic.

## Step 9: Disable Battery Saver

Battery saver mode can block VPN apps in the background.

On Android, disable battery optimization for your VPN app.

## Step 10: Contact Your Provider

If nothing works, your VPN account may be expired or the server may be blocked.

Ask your provider for:

- New subscription link
- New server profile
- Updated QR code
- Recommended app settings

## Final Thoughts

Most VPN connection problems can be fixed by:

1. Checking internet.
2. Changing server.
3. Updating subscription.
4. Restarting the app.
5. Trying another protocol.

If your VPN still does not connect, contact your provider for fresh server details.
