## Overview

Public Wi-Fi is convenient, but it can also be risky.

Networks in cafes, airports, hotels, and shopping centers may expose your data if they are not properly secured.

Using a VPN on public Wi-Fi helps protect your connection and reduce privacy risks.

## Why Public Wi-Fi Can Be Risky

Public Wi-Fi networks are often shared by many people.

Risks include:

- Fake Wi-Fi networks
- Data interception
- Tracking
- Unsecured websites
- Session hijacking
- Malicious hotspot attacks

A VPN encrypts your traffic and makes it harder for others on the same network to monitor your activity.

## Step 1: Connect to the Correct Wi-Fi

Before connecting, confirm the official Wi-Fi name.

Ask staff if you are in a cafe, hotel, or airport.

Avoid networks with suspicious names like:

- Free_Public_WiFi
- Airport_Free_Unlimited
- Cafe_Guest_5G_Official_2

Fake networks often use names that look real.

## Step 2: Turn On Your VPN

Open your VPN app and connect before logging into websites or apps.

Choose a nearby server for better speed.

![Connect VPN on public Wi-Fi](/uploads/public-wifi-connect-vpn.jpg)

## Step 3: Avoid Sensitive Actions Without VPN

Do not log into banking, email, or business accounts before the VPN is connected.

Wait until the VPN app shows **Connected**.

## Step 4: Enable Kill Switch

If your VPN app supports kill switch, enable it.

This blocks internet if the VPN disconnects.

It helps prevent your real IP and traffic from leaking.
